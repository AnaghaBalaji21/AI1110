\textbf{Question: 12.13.1.14}
Given that the two numbers appearing on throwing two dice are different. Find the probability of the event the sum of numbers in the dice is 4\\ 
\textbf{Solution: }\\
The total number of outcomes when throwing two dice = 6*6 = 36\\
Outcomes where the two numbers appearing on the dice are different and sum is 4:\\
(1,3) ; (3,1)\\
\\
Conditional Probability:\\
If event A occurs given that the event B has already occured then,\\
\begin{align}
	\pr{A|B} = \frac{\pr{AB}}{\pr{B}}
\end{align}
Here,\\
A: Sum of numbers on two dice is 4\\ 
B: Numbers on the two dice are different\\
We know,\\
\begin{align}
	\pr{AB} = \frac{2}{36}
\end{align}
Outcomes on the dice are different:
\begin{align}
	\pr{B} = \frac{6*5}{36} = \frac{30}{36} 
\end{align}
Conditional Probabiity \pr{A|B}:
\begin{align}
	\pr{A|B} &= \frac{\pr{A.B}}{\pr{B}}\\
	\pr{A|B} &= \frac{\frac{2}{36}}{\frac{30}{36}}\\ 
	\pr{A|B} &= \frac{2}{30}\\
	\pr{A|B} &= \frac{1}{15}
\end{align}
$\therefore$ The probability of the event the sum of the dices is 4 given that the two dices show different number is $\frac{1}{15}$.
